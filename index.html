Beginners · Chapter 1

What Is Investing?

The essential vocabulary every investor needs to know.

Investing (השקעות) means putting your money into something with the expectation it will grow over time. Instead of leaving cash sitting idle, investors put it to work in markets, businesses, or assets.

The person who invests money is called an Investor (משקיע). When you invest, you expect to receive a Return (תשואה) — the percentage gain or income your investment earns.

Investing often happens through the Stock Market / Bourse (בורסה), a marketplace where buyers and sellers trade financial assets. The most famous is the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE).

מניה
Stock / Share
A small ownership piece of a company. If the company grows, your stock rises in value.
תיק השקעות
Portfolio
Your entire collection of investments — stocks, bonds, real estate, and more.
רווח
Profit
Money earned when your investment grows in value or pays income.
הפסד
Loss
Money lost when your investment falls in value below what you paid.
💡

Beginner tip: You don't need thousands of dollars to start investing. Many platforms allow you to start with as little as $1–10 in fractional shares.

Beginners · Chapter 2

Risk & Diversification

Understanding how to manage risk in your portfolio.

Risk (סיכון) is the possibility that your investment will lose value. All investments carry some level of risk. The key insight: higher potential return usually means higher risk.

Volatility (תנודתיות) measures how much an asset's price swings up and down. A volatile investment might jump 20% one month and drop 15% the next. Stable investments like government bonds have low volatility.

The smartest way to manage risk is through Diversification (פיזור סיכונים) — spreading your money across different types of investments so that if one drops, others may hold steady or rise.

Risk levels by investment type
Government BondsLow
Large Company Stocks (ETF)Medium
Individual StocksMedium-High
CryptocurrencyVery High
⚠️

Never invest money you cannot afford to lose. Markets go up and down. A diversified portfolio helps protect you from any single bad investment wiping you out.

Beginners · Chapter 3

Types of Investments

The main asset classes every investor should understand.

There are four main types of investments. Each has different risk levels, returns, and purpose in a portfolio.

קרן סל
ETF
Exchange Traded Fund — a basket of stocks you can buy like a single share. Great for beginners since it gives instant diversification.
אג״ח
Bonds
A loan you give to a company or government. They pay you interest over time. Lower risk than stocks, lower return.
נדל״ן
Real Estate
Owning property to earn rental income or profit from rising prices. Can also invest via REITs (real estate funds) on the stock market.
מטבעות דיגיטליים
Cryptocurrency
Digital currencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum. Very high risk and volatility, but some investors allocate a small portion here.

A smart portfolio often combines several of these types. For example: 60% ETFs, 30% bonds, 10% real estate. This classic approach balances growth with stability.

💡

ETFs are widely considered the best starting point for beginner investors. They're low-cost, diversified, and require no expertise in picking individual stocks.

Advanced Concepts

The concepts that separate good investors from great ones.

Compound Interest (ריבית דריבית) is the most powerful force in investing. You earn interest on your original money — and then interest on the interest you already earned. Over decades, this creates exponential growth.

Compound Interest Example

on $1,000 @ 5%
After 20 years
$2,653
on $1,000 @ 5%
After 40 years
$7,040
on $1,000 @ 5%
ניתוח טכני
Technical Analysis
Studying price charts and patterns to predict future price movements. Used by traders.
ניתוח פונדמנטלי
Fundamental Analysis
Evaluating a company's real value — profits, debt, growth — to decide if it's a good investment.
שווי שוק
Market Cap
Total value of a company's shares. Price per share × number of shares = market capitalization.
דיבידנד
Dividend
Cash paid to shareholders from a company's profits. Some investors live off dividend income.
אינפלציה
Inflation
The rising cost of goods over time. Your money loses purchasing power. Investing helps beat inflation.
Tips & Guide · Chapter 5

Investing Strategies

Practical principles every investor should follow.

Here are the time-tested principles that guide successful investors — regardless of whether you're just starting out or have years of experience.

🎯
Start Early

Time is your greatest advantage. Due to compound interest, ₪1,000 invested at age 25 is worth far more than ₪2,000 invested at age 45.

📊
Diversify

Never put all your money in one stock or one sector. Spread across different assets, industries, and geographies.

📅
Invest Regularly

Use Dollar-Cost Averaging — invest a fixed amount every month regardless of price. This reduces the impact of market volatility.

🧘
Stay Calm

Markets drop. It's normal. The biggest mistake beginners make is panic-selling during downturns. Long-term investors who stay the course win.

📚
Keep Learning

The more you understand investing, the better decisions you make. Keep reading, learning, and refining your strategy over time.

✨

The best investment you can make is in your own financial education. The knowledge you build here will compound just as powerfully as your money.
